Piney IPA Recipe

By Shane English from Real Simple Brewing

Ingredients

Instructions

Steeping grains

  • 0.75 lb Crystal 70L
  • 0.25 lb Honey
  • 0.10 lb Biscuit

Steeping in 1 gallon for 30 minutes in the range of 146°-154° F

The Boil

Start with 6.5 gallons of water in the boiling kettle. After water is boiling, add steeped grain water, 8 lbs of liquid extract (6.72 lbs of dry malt extract) and 0.75 lb of corn sugar. Once it is boiling it is time to start the 90 minute timer.

  • 2oz of Columbus @ 90 minutes left
  • 1oz of Columbus @ 45 minutes left
  • 1oz of Simcoe @ 30 minutes left
  • 2oz of Simcoe when fire is turned off
  • 1oz of Centennial when fire is turned off

Force chill, must go down to below 75° F. Make sure that everything is clean and sanitized. Syphon all the beer from the kettle. We will be adding Ale yeast from white labs, either with a starter or with an additional vial (2 total). Must be shaken or use an oxygen stone to airate the solution.

Take the original gravity reading and maybe a small taste of the beer. 3 days later or after primary fermentation has subdue, add dry hop ingredients.

  • 1oz of Simcoe
  • 1oz of Centennial
  • 1oz of Columbus

Directly into the carboy.

Two weeks from brew day, take a look at the final gravity. The OG aim was 1.066, FG was 1.012, with about 7.1 % ABV. Then the bottling can begin. Make sure that the keg is clean.
